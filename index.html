"Journal Entry — Washington D.C."
June 3-5, 2026
On the first destination of our trip, we headed to multiple museums and enjoyed the history of our country. Places like the Lincoln Memorial, Vietnam Veterans Memorial, and Washington Monument were all touching and beautiful to view for the first time. After that, we headed to the National Mall food trucks for lunch. Afterwards, we ended our Washington section with the Smithsonian Museums, which had amazing historical artifacts like the first American flag that spanned 30 by 42 feet. After this, we headed to New York via an Amtrak and enjoyed seeing the Brooklyn Bridge in the evening. All in all, our first two stops were great ways to start our trip, and we enjoyed it thoroughly.
APHuG Connections
Unit 4
Forward Capital
DC was purpose-built on neutral land between Northern & Southern states. Neither Philadelphia nor New York was given an advantage.
Unit 4
No Primate City
The USA follows the rank-size rule. Political power is equally dispersed across NYC, LA, Chicago, and Houston. No single city dominates all functions.
Unit 3
UN Language: English
English is one of 6 official UN languages. The USA is the world's largest English-speaking country by GDP and cultural output. However, it technically isn't the USA's official language.
Unit 2
DTM Stage 4–5
Low birth rate, low death rate, aging population, near-zero natural increase. Net IN-migration country. The immigration in America is what mainly sustains population growth. These factors all lead the US to become a stage 4-5 country.

APHuG Connections
Unit 1
Caribbean Region
Trinidad and Tobago fulfills the Caribbean regional requirement. These countries are distinct from South America despite the geographic proximity between the locations. Trinidad and Tobago is also an island state with unique cultural geography.
Unit 3
Religious Diversity
Christianity (~65%), Hinduism (~22%), and Islam (~5%) all coexist with each other, resulting in African, Indian, and European population mixing. It also has a syncretic cultural landscape.
Unit 7
Petroleum Export
Pointe-à-Pierre refinery: oil & gas = #1 export. Location due to offshore field proximity and coastal infrastructure. It has a very resource-oriented industry.
Unit 2
DTM Stage 3 + Out-migration
Declining birth rates, low death rates. Significant emigration to the UK, USA, and Canada is leading to "brain drain" patterns, which are very common in small Caribbean nations.

APHuG Connections
Unit 4
Primate City
Montevideo holds around 54% of Uruguay's population and produces 65% of GDP, which are all very textbook examples of primate city dominance in a small unitary state.
Unit 3
UN Language: Spanish
Spanish is one of the 6 UN official languages. Uruguay's Spanish reflects Rioplatense dialect influence and is distinct from Castilian or Mexican Spanish.
Unit 6
Typical Home
Punta Carretas: Art Deco & modernist apartment blocks represent typical Uruguayan middle-class urban housing, not folk, having vernacular contemporary style.
Unit 2
DTM Stage 4–5
Uruguay has a low birth rate, low death rate, and aging population, making it one of Latin America's most demographically advanced nations. Uruguay also shows net in-migration.

APHuG Connections
Unit 4
Primate City
Paris is a primate city because its population is much larger than that of the next biggest city, Lyon. In fact, the population of Paris is almost 5.5 times larger than that of Lyon, with Paris at ~12M and Lyon at ~2.2M.
Unit 3
UN Language: French
France's official language, French, is one of the 6 official languages in the UN. The historical language of France actually acts as a centripetal cultural force.
Unit 7
Renault Flins
We went to an auto assembly plant. We observed that, though it was automated, there was a skilled labor pool, a transport corridor, and a supplier cluster in Île-de-France.
Unit 6
Typical Home
The typical home in France follows an architecture called Haussmann. There was a Haussmann apartment block with 6-story limestone, uniform cornices, and wrought iron balconies. They were built in the ~1850s-1870s.
